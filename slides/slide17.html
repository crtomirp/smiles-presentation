Handling Charged Molecules and Ions

Brackets for Special Cases

Square brackets `[]` are used to enclose atoms that are in a non-standard state. This includes atoms with a formal charge, a specific isotopic mass, or an unusual valence state.

Charges are indicated with `+` or `-`. For charges greater than one, a number is used (e.g., `+2`). Unpaired electrons can also be specified.

Narration: SMILES can also represent ions. We use square brackets to specify an atom's charge. For the acetate anion, we wrap the negatively charged oxygen in brackets with a minus sign. For a simple cation like ammonium, the entire ion is placed in brackets with its positive charge, clearly defining it as a charged species.
