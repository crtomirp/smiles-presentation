Simple Rules to Build SMILES

A Guide to Molecular Line Notation

Presented by: Libby

Intended Learning Outcomes

  • Formulate valid SMILES strings, correctly representing atoms, bonds, branches, and rings.
  • Distinguish between canonical and non-canonical SMILES and explain the purpose of canonicalization.
  • Identify common errors in SMILES notation, such as incorrect syntax for aromaticity and ring closures.

Narration: Welcome. In this module, we'll explore the simple but powerful rules for representing complex chemical structures as a single line of text using a system called SMILES. By the end of this session, you will be able to construct valid SMILES strings for various molecules and understand the important difference between canonical and non-canonical forms. This skill is fundamental for working with chemical databases and computational tools.
