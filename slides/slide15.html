Specifying Double Bond Geometry (E/Z)

(E)-stilbene SMILES:

c1ccc(cc1)/C=C/c2ccccc2

(Z)-stilbene SMILES:

c1ccc(cc1)/C=C\c2ccccc2

Directional Bonds: "/" and "\"

To specify the geometry of substituents around a double bond, SMILES uses the "/" and "\" characters to indicate "up" and "down" directionality, respectively.

When these directional bonds are on the same side of the double bond (e.g., both up or both down), it indicates a trans or (E) configuration. When they are on opposite sides, it indicates a cis or (Z) configuration.

Narration: Let's add three-dimensional information to our strings. We can specify the cis/trans configuration of double bonds using forward and backward slashes. For (E)-stilbene, or trans-stilbene, notice how the slashes around the double bond are both forward slashes, indicating the phenyl groups are on opposite sides. For (Z)-stilbene, or cis-stilbene, we use one forward slash and one backward slash to show the groups are on the same side. This simple addition precisely defines the molecule's 3D shape.
