Defining Chiral Centers (R/S)

The "@" Symbol: Clockwise or Counter-Clockwise

The chirality at a tetrahedral atom (a chiral center) is specified using the "@" or "@@" symbols. These are placed directly after the atomic symbol for the chiral atom.

Looking from the previous atom in the string towards the chiral atom: - @ means the remaining three substituents are listed in a counter-clockwise order. - @@ means the substituents are listed in a clockwise order.

Narration: We can also specify the handedness of a chiral molecule. The "@" and "@@" symbols tell us the arrangement of atoms around a chiral center. For alanine, by changing from "@@" to "@", we define its mirror image. This allows us to distinguish between L-alanine, which is common in proteins, and its enantiomer, D-alanine.
